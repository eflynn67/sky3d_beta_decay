\doxysubsubsection{Detailed Description}
This module computes the total energy and the various contributions to it in two ways. The first method evaluates the density functional, by direct integration to compute the {\itshape  integrated energy} {\ttfamily ehfint}. The second method uses the sum of single-\/particle energies plus the rearrangement energies, $ E_{3,\rm corr} $ for the density dependent part and $ E_{C,\rm corr} $ for Coulomb exchange. 

The two ways of calculating the energy are assigned to the subroutines {\ttfamily integ\+\_\+energy}, which also calculates the rearrangement energies, and {\ttfamily sum\+\_\+energy}. Note that since {\ttfamily integ\+\_\+energy} is always called briefly before {\ttfamily sum\+\_\+energy}, the rearrangement energies are correctly available.

In addition subroutine {\ttfamily sum\+\_\+energies} also calculates the summed spin, orbital, and total angular momenta.

The different contribution to the total energy are
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item $ T $\+: the total kinetic energy calculated as \[ T=\sum_q \frac{\hbar^2}{2m_q}\int\D^3 r\, \tau_q \] with $ \tau_q $ the isospin-\/specific kinetic density.
\item $ E_0 $\+: The $ b_0 $ and $ b\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_0 $-\/dependent part is \[ E_0=\int \D^3\!r\,\left(\frac{b_0}{2}\rho^2-\frac{b_0\textnormal{\textquotesingle}}{2}\sum_q\rho_q^2\right). \]
\item $ E_{1}$\+: kinetic terms containing the coefficients $ b_1 $ and $ b_1\textnormal{\textquotesingle} $\+: \[ E_1=\int \D^3\!r\,\left(b_1[\rho\tau-{\vec\jmath\,}{}^2] -b_1\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\sum_q[\rho_q\tau_q-{\vec\jmath_q}{}^2]\right).\]
\item $ E_{2} $\+: terms containing the coefficients $ b_2 $ and $ b_2\textnormal{\textquotesingle} $. They involve the Laplacians of the densities. \[ E_2=\int \D^3\!r\,\left(-\frac{b_2}{2}\rho\Delta\rho +\frac{b_2\textnormal{\textquotesingle}}{2}\sum_q\rho_q\Delta\rho_q\right). \]
\item $ E_{3} $\+: The many-\/body contribution is given by \[ E_{3}=\int \D^3\!r\,\left(\frac{b_3}{3}\rho^{\alpha+2} -\frac{b_3\textnormal{\textquotesingle}}{3}\rho^\alpha\sum_q\rho_q^2\right). \]
\item $ E_\mathrm{ls} $\+: the spin-\/orbit energy \[ E_\mathrm{ls}=\int \D^3\!r\,\biggl( -b_4[\rho\nabla\cdot\vec J+ \vec s\cdot(\nabla\times\vec\jmath)]-b_4\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\sum_q[\rho_q\nabla\cdot\vec J_q+ \vec s_q\cdot(\nabla\times\vec\jmath_q)]\biggr) \]
\item $ E_\mathrm{C} $\+: the Coulomb energy. It consists of the standard expression for a charge distribution in its own field (Hartree term) plus the exchange term in the Slater approximation. The formula is \[ E_\mathrm{C} = \frac{e^2}{2}\int \D^3\!r \D^3\!r\textnormal{\textquotesingle} \frac{\rho_p(\vec{r})\rho_p(\vec{r\textnormal{\textquotesingle}})}{|\vec{r}-\vec{r\textnormal{\textquotesingle}}|} -\int \D^3\!r\,\frac{3e^2}{4}\left(\frac3{\pi}\right)^{\tfrac1{3}}\rho_p^{4/3} \] where $ e $ is the elementary charge with $ e^2=1.43989 $ MeV $ \cdot $fm.
\item $ E_\mathrm{pair} $\+: the pairing energy. It consists of a contact pairing interaction involving the pairing densities $ \xi_q $ augmented by an optional density dependence. The formula is \[ E_{\rm pair} = \frac{1}{4} \sum_{q\in\{p,n\}}V_\mathrm{pair,q} \int \D^3r |\xi_q|^2 \left[1 -\frac{\rho}{\rho_{0,\mathrm{pair}}}\right]\;. \] It contains a continuous switch, the parameter $ \rho_{0,\mathrm{pair}} $. A pure $ \delta $-\/interaction (DI), also called volume pairing, is recovered for $ \rho_{0,\mathrm{pair}}\longrightarrow\infty $. The general case is the density dependent $ \delta $-\/interaction (D\+D\+DI). A typical value near matter equilibrium density $ \rho_{0,\mathrm{pair}}=0.16 $ fm $ ^{-3} $ concentrates pairing to the surface. The most flexible choice is to consider $ \rho_{0,\mathrm{pair}} $ as an additional free parameter. Actual adjustments with this option deliver a form of the pairing functional which stays in between the extremes of volume and surface pairing. 
\end{DoxyItemize}

{\footnotesize\ttfamily subroutine energies\+::integ\+\_\+energy}



The purpose of this subroutine is to calculate the integrated energy. 

This is implemented pretty straightforwardly. The only programming technique worth noting is that intermediate variables such as {\ttfamily rhot} for the total density are used to avoid repeating the lengthy index lists. Compilers will eliminate these by optimization.

In principle the integration loops in the subroutine could be combined, but some space is saved by using the array {\ttfamily worka} for different purposes in different loops.

The calculation proceeds in the following steps\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item {\bfseries{ Step 1\+:}} the Laplacian of the densities is calculated in {\ttfamily worka}, then the integrals for {\ttfamily ehf0},{\ttfamily ehf2}, and {\ttfamily ehf3} are performed. After the loop the result for {\ttfamily ehf3} is also used to calculate {\ttfamily e3corr}.
\item {\bfseries{ Step 2\+:}} the integral for {\ttfamily ehf1} is evaluated using {\ttfamily worka} for the $ \vec\jmath_q{}^2 $ term.
\item {\bfseries{ Step 3\+:}} the spin-\/orbit contribution of {\ttfamily ehfls} is calculated using {\ttfamily worka} as storage for $ \nabla\cdot\vec J_q $.
\item {\bfseries{ Step 4\+:}} the Coulomb energy {\ttfamily ehfc} is evaluated with the Slater correction taken into account if the force\textquotesingle{}s {\ttfamily ex} is nonzero. At the same time the Coulomb correction for the summed energy is calculated and stored in {\ttfamily ecorc}. It will be used in the subroutine {\ttfamily sum\+\_\+energy}.
\item {\bfseries{ Step 5\+: }} the kinetic energy is integrated for {\ttfamily ehft}. Note that only at this point the correct prefactor $ \hbar^2/2m $ is added; the use of {\ttfamily tau} in other expressions assumes its absence.
\item {\bfseries{ Step 6\+: }} Finally all terms are added to produce the total energy, {\ttfamily efundet}, from which the pairing energies are subtracted. 
\end{DoxyItemize}\mbox{\Hypertarget{namespaceenergies_a1dc9b0b174ca7597ab4902cf0c555d94}\label{namespaceenergies_a1dc9b0b174ca7597ab4902cf0c555d94}}

\doxyparagraph{\texorpdfstring{sum\_energy()}{sum\_energy()}}
{\footnotesize\ttfamily subroutine energies\+::sum\+\_\+energy}



This subroutine mainly computes the Koopman sum, but also sums up a number of other single-\/particle properties. 

For systematics, the latter should be done in a different place, but at present is left here.

The summation of the total energy uses {\ttfamily spenerg} to compute \[ \sum_k (\epsilon_k-\tfrac1{2}v_k)=\tfrac1{2}\sum_k(2t_k+v_k)= \tfrac1{2}\sum_k(t_k+\epsilon_k). \] The last sum is calculated, the rearrangement corrections are added and the pairing energies subtracted.

The subroutine then sums up the single-\/particle energy fluctuation {\ttfamily sp\+\_\+efluct1} and {\ttfamily sp\+\_\+efluct2}, dividing them by the nucleon number. Finally the orbital and spin angular momentum components are summed to form the total ones.
